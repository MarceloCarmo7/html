Content

Home page

Pet owners find difficult in making a trip or leaving their houses for too long because they are concerned about their pets situations. It is not always possible to bring a pet with in while traveling, for example, would a family bring one or more pets to their vacations in Las Vegas? The answer is probably not. Petland exists for people who want to travel with peace of mind and no concerns if their pets are okay or not.

Dietary

Just like people, pets need food that meets their specific nutritional needs. Dog foods are designed to meet a dog's nutritional need and cat foods are designed to meet a cat's nutritional needs.That's Why We take care that not only do they remain separated but they do not eat each other's food. Importantly, their dietary needs are very different. Puppies require different food from senior dogs. Pets with health issues may require special diets. Table scraps are typically not good for dogs and cats because many of the foods we eat, such as salt, garlic, and onions are bad for your pet's health and can make them sick or even be fatal.

If your pet lives in water, like fish and turtles, We clean our aquariums at least once a week!

Socialization

The environment between pets is vital for their humor, specially when they are still puppies.

Our pet Hotel is comfortable and safe. There are many activities during the day which will prevent stress in those little cuties and balanced meals as well. Daily exercise is very important whether you own a cat or a dog. This is what helps give them physical and mental stimulation. Many pets need at least an hour of exercise daily to stay physically and mentally healthy. Here are some examples in our Hotel:

  • Provide your pet with comfortable and safe lodging and nutritious meals.
  • Give them plenty of exercise and socialization opportunities. This includes playtime with other pets, as well as supervised walks.
  • Provide professional pet sitting and care for each pet 24/7, ensuring their safety at all times.
  • Administer any necessary medications or treatments according to a vet's instructions.
